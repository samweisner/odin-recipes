Tortizza Recipe

Description

The tortizza is one idea in a seemingly endless pool of options to pick from for a quick lunch or weeknight dinner. For me, it epitomizes low-effort, high-reward cooking on those days when a big dinner project is just not going to happen. The end result is a crispy, thin-crust tavern-style pie that rivals some of the artisinal Midwest pizzerias' products at a fraction of the price -- and in hardly no time at all.

Ingredients

  • Stacey's Organic 10" Wheat Flour Tortillas
  • Muir Glen Organic Pizza Sauce
  • Whole Milk Mozzarella Cheese
  • Monterey Jack Cheese
  • Pizza Toppings of Choice

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to around 425°F (ovens vary, so you could really go +/-25° and adapt from there).
  2. Grab a flour tortilla and splash a bit of olive oil (1-2 tsp) on each side, gently rubbing it in for even coverage.
  3. Pull your Muir Glen Organic Pizza Sauce out of the cupboard, crack it open, and spread a thin layer of sauce over the tortilla, leaving a half-inch unsauced border for the crust. You can season the sauce at this point with some crushed chili flakes, fennel seeds and dried oregano.
  4. Using a box grater placed over a bowl, coarsely shred around three parts Mozzarella to one part Monterey Jack and mix together.
  5. Sprinkle shredded cheese over the sauce until covered, then dot with your favorite toppings and a drizzle of olive oil. If using sausage, please par-fry it first!
