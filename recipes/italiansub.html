Italian Sub Recipe

Description

I like Jimmy John's and Jersey Mike's subs, but I don't like the preservatives they use in most (if not all) of their ingredients. So if you want to eat Italian subs for dayzzz (and trust me, you will after trying this recipe), stock up on ingredients and you'll end up saving a hell of a lot of money (think $2.50 per sub instead of $8-10). Grab a bag of chips (I like jalapeno or salt & vinegar), wash it all down with a sparkling citrus flavored water, and whip up a batch of homemade chewy chocolate chip cookies for dessert. Blam! Jimmy John's at home.

Ingredients

  • Onion
  • Tomato
  • Red Wine Vinegar
  • Dried Oregano
  • French Bread or Hoagie Rolls
  • Provolone Slices
  • Mustard & Mayo
  • Olive Oil
  • Iceberg Lettuce
  • Sliced Italian Cured Meats
  • Giardiniera or Sliced Pickled Peppers

Steps

  1. Slice the onion thin, rinse under cold water, then add to a bowl with vinegar, oregano, and a pinch of salt. Stir and let sit.
  2. Slice and toast the rolls in a low oven if desired. Spread mustard & mayo on each cut side (optional).
  3. Shingle the surface with cheese, then the cured meats, then the pickled items.
  4. Add shredded lettuce and a splash of olive oil to the pickling onions, tossing until mixed.
  5. Pile the veggies (including thinly sliced tomato) on one side of the sandwich, then close up the bread and push down lightly. Consume immediately.
